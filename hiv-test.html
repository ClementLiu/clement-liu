HIV test application

It is a project for our health care program. Our mission was to design a mobile application for Oraquick In-Home HIV Test, a top selling self test kit for potential HIV to provide a more friendly and effective instruction for users.

Roles

Responsible for interface iteration and visual design

Duration

5 weeks

Oraquick test kit

Oraquick is the first in-home oral HIV test brand. Oraquick test kit can provide HIV test result within 30 mins in the privacy of user’s own home. Also it don’t involved any blood since it’s an oral test and just needs swap the gums.

It is a well designed test kit. Beside oral test sticks and tube, it has step-by-step directions, holder to hold the tools, and information booklet on HIV testing. You can buy this test kit from most retailers, like Walmart, CVS pharmacy, and amazon. Privacy is a big concern for potential HIV patients, that’s the reason for it’s a self test kit that can be easily used at home. And! It also contains an opaque disposal bag for this reason. Good job Oraquick!

App for test kit

Great HIV test kit but not good enough

The physical HIV test kit does pretty well on the examine functions. It’s fast and convent. But for the HIV potential users, the emotional support would be the kicker. Here is the serious part. We have to realize the users are holding a lot of pressure. Most of them might be the first time doing HIV test in their whole life, and facing this vital disease along, without any professional people around. The test can easily go wrong. Because of the particularity of HIV and the social attitude to this disease, that whoever got this disease must have some dissolute lifestyle and the death might spread to others, these prejudices and lack of sufficient information would leave heavy burden on the patients and drown users in helplessness. Not to mention if the result is positive, besides facing the shocking fact, the user need more. Helpful support and correct information about HIV would be needed comforts. That’s what app can contribute to the HIV test experience.

Instead of facing the fear of HIV alone, utilizing test applicant lead to a more positive testing experience.

Pro:

Fast, get result with in 30 min

Convent, no blood need

Private, solo job and easily dispose

Con:

Strict, mistakes lead to fault results

Loneliness, face heavy fact alone

Aloof, limited care for stressful user

Hopeless, lack next step for bad result

What if we can companion user with sufficient emotionally / information support while taking the test.

Relative services

Because of the particularity of HIV, patient seldom learn HIV from family or friends around. But good news is there are many well-documented HIV platforms out there provided by government or organizations, like American centers for disease and control. User can find all details and facts about what’s HIV, why you need test, transmission of HIV, and so on. After spend 1 hour on studying it, you feel you are like a HIV doctor can solve many many questions about it, which are similar to the heavy text booklet in test kit. But it’s not enough. That’s why there are community support. On these platform, real human with HIV share their life story about how they tell HIV to their family. Here you will find how to keep patients positive by knowing there are someone standing out there don’t want you to be helpless.

  • Easy consuming useful information
  • Supports from who understand each other
Persona

After listening to the story of people having HIV, I understood the difficulty in taking the test the social pressure and unknown future. User wants to have complete privacy about the HIV test before they figure out how to handle the situation with anyone else. The situation can be very complex, might include pregnant, marriage, betrayal, and break up. Telling users a positive result is like throwing tornado into their life. A little warm suggestion and hope for tomorrow would make big different.

Health Apps Regulations

After reading the very-rightful instructions, I had to say I felt it is very solid legal text to prevent any possible legal issue the test might raise. I believe it is appropriate in medial product. It raise my concern about the regulation in designing a medial application. After some research I found these kind of application don’t need to submit pre-market review or to register and list their apps with the FDA, which set a playgroundfor us to build better user experience without highly restricted regulation.

  • Help patients/users self-manage their disease or condition without providing specific treatment suggestions;
  • Provide patients with simple tools to organize and track their health information;
  • Provide easy access to information related to health conditions or treatments;
  • Help patients document, show or communicate potential medical conditions to health care providers;
  • Automate simple tasks for health care providers;
  • Enable patients or providers to interact with Personal Health Records (PHR) or Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems.

Our Oraquick test kit app can easily fit in this category.

The opportunities

This test kit is pretty thoughtful and proved to be helpful for two decades. But there are many opportunities aviliable for digit application.

Heavy text

The direction is text heavy and difficult to follow each steps. Especially it is a single-use test kit. Any mistake in process can ruin the result. 15 min I spent to read all of them to be confident enough to start use it.

Anxiety

Taking HIV test is stressful and anxious. The test kit is trying to soothe users through directions and words, which can be improved a lot through application.

Diagram mixed with direction

The illustration looks good but there are still many details in directions, which can be easily missed, like be ready to use timer, swap only once, etc.

Lack hierarchy

It contains serval different booklets for test direction, HIV Information, medical fact for test tools, packaging list, legal information, and warning. Users have to figure out which they should start reading.

Easily ignored help information

There are some next step for HIV positive test. These helping gestures can be more useful and efficient in application.

Re-organize the information flow

The current Oraquick test kit user experience is very overloaded for user to learn especially at the moment taking HIV diagnosis. It wants the user to know everything important or not important right now before they start doing test. Most of the information do not need to be consumed all at once. The test kit does a really good job in covering every possible situations through the test and for various users. In test kid application there are chance we can make the instruction more related to specific part of the test.

As you can seen in the test flow below, the original test process can be separated into several parts. Before test, preparing for test, during the test, waiting for result, negative results, positive result etc.

Don't need to read all of them, but to read and learn along with the test.

Before test, user have no idea about how this test will go but they have decided to jump in the test, at this moment, we only need to show basic requirements and whole view about the test. We can remove why you should take test and how you get HIV to the package.

Preparing for test, besides the whole time needed for the test, user need to be ready for preventing anything might causing false result, like no test within 30 mins after using any mouth wash product.

During the test, user need clear and detailed instructions for each steps. Application can reduce the tedious tasks for users to cut down the pressure on them.

Waiting for result,user have to wait for 20 mins for the result. Before 20 mins or exceed 40 mins, the result will not be valid. In this period, it is an empty time slot in test kit but its is an opportunity for user to have some mental rehearsal for the possible bad results. We can provide helpful information to calm user down and response to HIV in a more thoughtful way.

Get result,here can provide necessary knowledge in reliable information and emotional support for the customers. We can specific help for various group of users, pregnant female, male, or immaturity. And also the community nearby would reduce the helplessness.

Initial idea

I aimed to ideate on humanizing onboarding experience. Take it easy also calm down our users. I have ideas that turn heavy text into step by step talking. Instead of putting a page of instruction text in-front of user’s face, the heavy reading work can be turned into a warm chatting between user and application and give more specified instruction for user.

Narrow down

Turing heavy text into one by one dialogs makes the process easier than just reading. I decide to focus the dialogue instead of personification because I realized the first impression of chatting interface is realtime and non-retractable which also increase the pressure of not making mistake. From the initial concept, collecting necessary information can be used to customize the instruction. But it created a dilemma between privacy and personalization. I felt that the value of knowing what user need and provide specific support would be important in reducing the helplessness, particularly providing other patients' stories. The challenge was asking the question at the right moments.

User flow

By inspecting the user flow for the success user group, we target three moments of asking questions. The first is basic information for starting test, which is optional and less related to the user. As process advance, there is an inevitable 20 min waiting time for the test result. User are ready the see the brief facts about HIV and be introduced with other support material in application. As user has known the results and stepped on foot into the process, user are more likely to tell more situation about themselves for more specific helps.

Information architecture

Based on the user flow and content, I proposed a potential Information architecture. I categorized three main part of the application, the test instruction, HIV facts, and community service, and profile.

Low-fi wireframes

We integrated main functions into 4 categories and two user flows. One is for the first time user, who will go through test instruction, and second users go and find saved articles, videos and doctors in their using history.

Accomplished task

The task progress section would tell how many steps left to be done, and indicate what’s next task to explore. Each task progress section starts with a task already completed to encourage engagement.

Timer/reminder

The timer and reminder function would reduce stress from user. The test time and date is critical for the test accuracy. Instead of letting user take a note or watch clock on the phone, which they might forget, the build in timer lift the burden from them.

Care privacy

Privacy, privacy, privacy. It’s never enough to take care about privacy in HIV test situation. A little sloppy notification would leak the personal information. The notification setting appear after showing how the notification will looks like to reduce the possibility of being seen by others.

Step by step instructions

The last step in test instruction is the most stressful moment. Instead of just telling which pattern is positive, we let user to chose the pattern first to reduce emotional bias and use two step confirmation, select and submit to reduce false result from mistake.

Human Help

After rapid test, we realized that users have various situations. At the same time there are many policy details response to different situations they might face, like house support, medical cost waive, pregnant support, transportation help, etc. And thought about our initial goal is to provide sufficient emotionally / information support which means prepare them for the next steps. Instead of keep them in the application with bunch of information, it would be better to lead user out of application to find the right people provide specific help and support.

Visual components

From the visual mock up, we organized them into several different pages structure which can be the template for other pages. We defined the visual language as trustful and friendly. We borrowed the format of prescription as our visual reference. Then with designing these pages we settled down the design system.
