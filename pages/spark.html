EMOTION : RAWWWW!

I was required to use RAW as the word to design and interpret it with form and way of using. I designeda raw fly swatter, which uses fire to kill flies, with stake appearance, just like the way we can kill flies in the wild, Burning them!

INTRODUCTION

The development of technology and the popularization of electronic devices have separated people from nature. Everything around us is artificial, made for function and without conection to the cycle of life. It is essential to keep in mind that we are creatures with instincts. Spark is a “raw” fly killer that uses fire, one of our most ancient tools, to remind us of the ashes-to-ashes cycle

DEFINING RAW

I use images to define the meaning of raw. I collected suggestive images with search engines to illustrate my understanding of RAW.

ABSTRACT REFERENCE

Imagining I was in the forest and urgently wanted to kill these annoying flies, I came up with the following ideas. I made several clip art images to further explore and refine these ideas. After that, I made rapid mockups to compare and test the ideas.

LIZARD’S TONGUE

What animal preys on flies? It turns out to be lizards. Lizards catch flies with their long and sticky tongues. So I will use their tongues.

BURNING LOG

A traditional way to capture insects is to smear sticky honey on bark to attract them and prevent them from flying away.

GIANT HANDS

Why could I not just hit flies with my hands? Because they are too fast to be caught and small enough to slip away through the gaps between my fingers? So why not increase hand size?

VENUS FLYTRAP

One fly-killing plant, the Venus flytrap, with flowers as traps, inspired me to put the venus flytrap on my head and expectantly wait for the approaching fly sounds until they are trapped and the sounds disappear.

BURN LOG

The smell of burning flies reminds me of the feeling of rawness. And flies are one of the only things we can remind hunting again in today’s modern society.
